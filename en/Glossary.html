Glossary

Dream Manipulation:

The process of controlling and altering dreams, as presented in the movie Inception.

See more:

Totem:

A personal object used by characters in the film to distinguish reality from dreams based on its unique response in each state.

 See more :

Mind Projections:

In the context of the film, these are characters that appear in dreams, typically as adversaries or obstacles.

 See more :

Non-linear Plot:

A narrative structure where the story does not follow a classic chronological sequence but instead jumps in time or uses other techniques to create a complex narrative.

 See more :

Leitmotif:

A recurring theme or idea that appears regularly throughout a work, such as the phrase "N'aie pas peur de viser plus gros" in Inception.

 See more:

Real Stunts:

Action scenes and combat sequences involving actors performing real physical stunts instead of using special effects.

 See more:

Visionary:

A person who has the ability to see and conceive ideas or projects in an innovative and long-term way.

 See more:

Duality of Man:

The concept that humanity is inherently complex, with both dark and light aspects of human nature.

 See more:

Reverse Sequences:

Sequences in which the chronological order is reversed, so the beginning of the story is actually the end of the story.
