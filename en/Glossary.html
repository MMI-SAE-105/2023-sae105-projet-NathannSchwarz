Glossaire

Dream manipulation:

The process of controlling and modifying dreams, as presented in the film Inception.

Totem:

A personal object used by characters in the film to distinguish reality from dreams based on its unique response in each state.

Mind projections:

In the context of the film, these are the characters who appear in dreams, usually in the form of adversaries or obstacles.

Non-linear plot:

A narrative structure where the story doesn't follow a conventional chronological sequence, but instead jumps around in time or uses other techniques to create a complex narrative.

Leitmotiv:

A recurring theme or idea that recurs regularly throughout a work, such as the line "Don't be afraid to aim bigger" in Inception.

Real stunts:

Action scenes and fight sequences that involve actors performing real physical stunts instead of using special effects.

Visionary:

A person who has the ability to see and conceive of ideas or projects in an innovative, long-term way.

Duality of man:

The concept that humanity is intrinsically complex, with dark and light aspects of human nature.

Reverse sequences:

Sequences in which the chronological order is reversed, so that the beginning of the story is actually the end of the story.
